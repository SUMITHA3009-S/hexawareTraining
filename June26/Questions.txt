What is DevOps?
DevOps is a set of practices, cultural philosophies, and tools that unifies software Development (Dev) and IT Operations (Ops). 
It aims to shorten the development lifecycle and deliver high-quality software continuously by automating processes like building, testing, and deployment.
DevOps helps organizations evolve and improve products at a faster velocity than traditional software development methods.

Advantages of DevOps:
Faster delivery: Ships software updates and features much quicker.
Better collaboration: Breaks down silos between developers and operations.
Higher reliability: Reduces errors through automated testing and monitoring.
Easier scalability: Manages complex infrastructure efficiently using automated code.
Quicker recovery: Fixes bugs and system failures drastically faster.

What is agile methodology and what is scrum?
Agile is a flexible philosophy for managing projects through continuous, iterative improvement, 
while Scrum is a specific structured framework used to implement Agile.
AGILE: 
Agile is a project management approach centered on adaptability, collaboration, and delivering value to users in small, 
frequent increments rather than all at once.
SCRUME: 
Scrum is the most popular framework under the Agile umbrella. It organizes work into fixed, short timeframes and establishes 
strict team roles and daily meetings.

What are the important meetings in agile?
Sprint Planning: Held at the start to plan what to build and how.
Daily Standup: A 15-minute daily sync to track progress and flag roadblocks.
Sprint Review: Held at the end to demo working software to stakeholders.
Sprint Retrospective: Held at the end for the team to improve their workflow.
Backlog Refinement: Ongoing meeting to clarify and prepare future tasks.
